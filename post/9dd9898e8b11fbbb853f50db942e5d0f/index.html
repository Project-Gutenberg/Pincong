酒•白燈2020年11月4日於德拉華州威明頓市發表美國人民必勝演講

(他特別呼籲川建國與川粉們放下敵意：「我們不是敵人。」)

這裡是酒·白燈演講全文：

大家下午好！
賀錦麗和我在此先做個簡短聲明，我確信稍後的今晚或明天我們還有更多話要說，但首先請讓我感謝媒體諸君的光臨。
昨天，我親愛的美國同胞們，再次證明了民主是這個國家的心跳，就像兩個世紀來其一直是這個國家的心跳一樣。即使面對大疫情，仍有比以往任何時候都更多的美國人投票此次選舉。超過一億五千萬人民投出了他們的選票。我認為這實在太非同凡響了。如果我們對民主還懷有絲毫不信任，那就不配再擁有民有、民治和民享的政府了。民主生生不息，在美利堅生生不息。在這裡，人民行使治權。權力不可被強取豪奪亦不可被獨斷專行，它源自於人民，是人民的意志決定了誰將做合衆國總統，且其意志不受擺佈特立獨行。

[堅信我們必贏]

在一整夜點票之後，現在很明顯，我們贏得了足夠多的州，來達到勝選總統職位所需的270張選舉人票門檻。我不是在這裡宣布我們已經贏了，但我在此要報告一下，當計票完畢之時，相信我們必成贏家。在所有已點過的選票中，我們以二萬票贏得了威斯康星州的投票，幾乎與四年前川建國總統贏得該州的差額票數相同。在密歇根州，我們以超過三萬五千票領先且票數還在增長，這比川建國總統在2016年贏得密歇根州的票數差額都大得多。密歇根州將很快完成點票工作，也許最早就在今天。我對賓夕法尼亞州感到非常滿意。幾乎所有剩餘的待計票都是通過郵寄方式投送的。在賓夕法尼亞州，我們已贏得了78％的郵寄選票。我們翻轉了亞利桑那州和內布拉斯加州的第二區。對我來說，特別重要的是，我們已經贏得了美國人民的多數票，並且所有跡像都表明，(隨著計票點算的進行)多數票還會增長。

我們有著近三百萬票的廣爲人知的投票領先，並且所有跡像都表明該數字還將增長。实际上，賀錦麗參議員和我有望贏得比這個國家歷史上任何曾贏取總統與副總統職位的入場券都要多得多的票數，超過七千萬的票數。我為我們的競選感到十分驕傲。以往只有三場總統競選曾擊敗了現任總統。當此選舉完成之時，神意所屬，我們將成為第四個。這是一個重大成就。這是一個重大成就。

[誓做全民總統]

這是一個漫長而艱難的競選，於我國而言則是一個更艱難的時期，一個硬抗的時期。我們(國人)之前曾有過艱苦的競選。我們(同胞)之前曾面臨過硬抗的時期。
因此，一旦這場選舉完成並被抛諸身後，就該是做我們美國人一貫要做之事的時候了：將競選苛言拋諸腦後，壓下火氣，再度相視，再度互聽，再度彼此接受，彼此尊重相互關照，聯合，療愈，重新團結為一個國家。

我知道這並不容易。我不會很天真。我們都不天真。我深知我國内部在衆多事務上對立歧見的深度和硬度有多麽強烈。但我也十分明白這一點：要取得進步，我們必須停止將對手視為敵人。我們不是敵人。讓我們團結在一起而成爲美國人的力量，比任何能使我們分裂的力量，都要強大得多。
是故，誠教我以此拎清：我，我們，作爲民主黨人來參選，但我會以一個全美國人的總統身份來執政。
總統職位，其本身，並非一個黨派機構。而是代表著每一個人來行使權力並對全體國人都負有照顧職責的一個國家服務部門，這正是我將要做的(人民公僕工作)。對於那些沒投票支持我的人，我會像對那些投票支持我的人一樣，為他們而努力工作。

現在，每一選票都必須被點清。沒有人能將我們的民主從我們這裡奪走，現在不行，從來都不行。
美利堅途長行遠，美利堅身經百戰，美利堅受夠了任其胡作非爲。
我們人民，不會忍氣吞聲。我們人民，不會任憑欺凌。我們人民，不會認慫屈從。
朋友們，我堅信我們會贏得勝利。這將不僅僅是我個人的勝利，或僅只我們的勝利。而將是美國人民的勝利，我們民主的勝利，美利堅的勝利。我們獲勝之時，就不再有藍州和紅州了，而只有美利堅之團結諸州(美利堅合眾國)。
神佑諸君。神護吾軍。
謝謝！

Joe Biden addresses “It Will Be a Victory for the American People” speech on November 4, 2020, in Wilmington, Delaware

(He specially urges Build-the-Country Trump and trumpsters to give up enmity: “We are not enemies.”)

Here is Joe Biden’s speech in full:

Good afternoon, everyone.
Kamala and I we’re here to make a brief statement, and I’m sure we’ll have more to say later and either tonight or tomorrow, but let me begin by thanking the press for being here and thanking everyone else.
My fellow Americans, yesterday, once again proved that democracy is the heartbeat of this nation, just as it has been the heartbeat of this nation for two centuries. And even in the face of pandemic, more Americans voted this election than ever before in American history. Over 150 million people cast their votes. I think that’s just extraordinary. And if we had any doubts, we shouldn’t have any longer, about a government of, by, and for the people. It’s very much alive, very much alive in America. Here, the people rule. Power can’t be taken or asserted, it flows from the people, and it’s their will that determines who will be the president of the United States and their will alone.

[I Believe We Will Be the Winners]

And now after a long night of counting, it’s clear that we’re winning enough states to reach 270 electoral votes needed to win the presidency. I’m not here to declare that we’ve won, but I am here to report when the count is finished, we believe we will be the winners. Of all the votes counted, we have won Wisconsin by 20,000 votes, virtually the same margin that President Trump won that state four years ago. In Michigan, we lead by over 35,000 votes and it’s growing, a substantially bigger margin than President Trump won Michigan in 2016. Michigan will complete its vote soon, maybe as early as today. And I feel very good about Pennsylvania. Virtually all the remaining ballots to be counted are cast by mail. And we’ve been winning 78% of the votes by mail in Pennsylvania. We flipped Arizona and the second district of Nebraska. Of special significance to me is that we’ve won with the majority of the American people, and every indication is that the majority will grow.

We have a popular vote lead of nearly 3 million votes and every indication is that will grow as well. Indeed, Senator Harris and I are on track to win more votes than any ticket in the history of this country that ever won the presidency and vice presidency, over 70 million votes. I’m very proud of our campaign. Only three presidential campaigns in the past have defeated an incumbent president. When it’s finished, God willing, we’ll be the fourth. This is a major achievement. This is a major achievement.

[A President for All Americans]

It’s been a long and difficult campaign, but it’s been a more difficult time for our country, a hard time. We’ve had a hard campaigns before. We’ve faced hard times before.
So once this election is finalized and behind us, it will be time for us to do what we’ve always done as Americans: to put the harsh rhetoric of the campaign behind us, to lower the temperature, to see each other again, to listen to one another, to hear each other again, and respect and care for one another, to unite, to heal, to come together as a nation.

I know this won’t be easy. I’m not naive. Neither of us are. I know how deep and hard the opposing views are in our country on so many things. But I also know this as well: to make progress, we have to stop treating our opponents as enemies. We are not enemies. What brings us together as Americans is so much stronger than anything that can tear us apart. So let me be clear: I, we, are campaigning as a Democrats, but I will govern as an American president.
The presidency, itself, is not a partisan institution. It’s the one office in the nation that represents everyone and it demands a duty of care for all Americans, that is precisely what I will do. I will work as hard for those who didn’t vote for me as I will for those who did vote for me.

Now, every vote must be counted. No one is going to take our democracy away from us, not now, not ever.
America’s come too far. America’s fought too many battles. America’s endured too much to ever let that happen.
We the people, will not be silenced. We the people, will not be bullied. We the people, will not surrender.
My friends, I am confident we will emerge victorious, but this will not be my victory alone, or our victory alone. It will be a victory for the American people, for our democracy, for America. And there will be no blue states and red states when we win, just the United States of America.
God bless you all. And may God protect our troops.
Thank you.
